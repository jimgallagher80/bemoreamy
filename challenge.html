The Challenge

This is a 630-mile relay of the South West Coast Path, split into 68 legs and undertaken continuously, day and night, from 16 to 23 May 2026. Teams will take responsibility for individual legs, passing the baton from one group to the next at designated handover points along the route.

The route is usually completed anti-clockwise. We will be travelling in the opposite direction, starting at Studland at 8am on 16 May and finishing at Bossington around noon on 23 May.

The challenge is being undertaken in memory of Cdr Amy Gilmore MBE. Amy had planned a sabbatical to complete the South West Coast Path herself, raising money for charity following the loss of her father shortly before she was diagnosed with cancer. She found strength and enjoyment in walking sections of the path with friends and family during her treatment. Although she did not have the opportunity to complete it, this relay will carry forward that intention.

Amy’s walking sticks will act as the baton, passed from team to team at each leg handover.

Team leaders can sign up for their chosen leg or legs via the website. Detailed team member information can be submitted afterwards using the follow-up link provided once a leg is confirmed.

The timings shown on the route page are estimated. While the event is underway, live progress updates will be available on the website. We ask teams to be flexible with start times, as coastal terrain and conditions may affect progress.

Teams should arrive at their specified handover location ready to begin at the estimated time, unless they are completing consecutive legs, in which case they may continue without a formal handover.

Participants must be self-sufficient and bring their own food and water. A suggested kit list will be provided by the organisers. Support vehicles and crew will operate along sections of the route, but this cannot be guaranteed. Teams should plan to complete their leg independently.

If a leg is already taken, you are still encouraged to get involved. You may contact us and we will pass your details to the team assigned to that leg. Alternatively, you are welcome to attend a handover point and walk alongside participants. The event is open to all and we want as many people as possible to take part.
